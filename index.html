The $32 Gaming PC

Most people think PC gaming is an expensive hobby, but this guy built a working gaming PC for 32 bucks. This Redditor snagged a Dell workstation case for seven bucks from Value Village, which included a Xeon processor, 16 gigs of DDR4 RAM, and a mystery motherboard. The GPU was the legendary GTX 980 Ti, aka the card that once ruled benchmarks and apparently now sits on Facebook Marketplace for the price of a Chipotle burrito. Originally listed at 30 bucks, he talked the seller down to 15 bucks for a maybe broken 980 Ti. That just proves you can still win in the PC world, even if your rig looks like it came out of a garage sale boss.

Budget-build follow-up: half the price, same bit.

Video script · tech news

RIP Windows 10

Launched in 2015, Windows 10 was supposed to be… the LAST version of Windows. At least that's what Microsoft said! No Windows 11. Just updates forever. It brought back the Start Menu, snapped windows like a boss, and secretly hid a full-on "God Mode" folder that unlocked EVERY setting on your PC. Oh, and that default wallpaper? Was made with real lasers and smoke machines. It wasn't CGI. It was ART. 10 years. 1 billion devices. From Minecraft to crashing updates… Windows 10 wasn't perfect. But it was home.

End-of-an-era send-off for a ten-year OS.

The $100 Gaming PC

This 12-year-old’s gaming PC was lost in a house fire last year, but no worries, his uncle had his back. With ONLY 100 DOLLARS, unc took 6 months to assemble the deal of a lifetime. Sure, the case has seen better days and it’s hooked up to just a 900p monitor, but for a hundred bucks you’re still getting triple-digit frames, and that’s a bargain any way you slice it. Is it gonna run Cyberpunk Ultra? Probably not. But for a 12-year-old on a budget, this rig is absolute gold.

Wholesome budget-build story.

Loudest Gaming PC Ever

Forget about PC liquid cooling. How about a PC case made out of fans? This Redditor took cooling to the next level by mounting 38 fans directly onto their PC case. Not only is this PC going to run ice cold, but every time it powers on, the dude is basically throwing a full-blown RGB rave in his room. With 38 fans spinning at full blast, that PC isn’t just cooled, it’s freaking screaming. Don’t try this unless you’re okay gaming in a wind tunnel. Seriously.
